# 🧠 Artificial Intelligence (AI) Special Interest Group (SIG)

The **AI Special Interest Group (SIG)** supports collaboration among employees working with artificial intelligence across projects by providing a lightweight forum to exchange ideas, experiences, and practical solutions.

The group focuses on:

* Sharing lessons learned across AI initiatives
* Discussing implementation challenges and troubleshooting technical issues
* Identifying reusable approaches and internal best practices
* Hosting informal knowledge-sharing sessions and short technical presentations
* Strengthening internal expertise in the responsible and effective application of AI
* Reducing duplicated effort and improving delivery efficiency

## Topics

Within the SIG, **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** refers to computational approaches—primarily **machine learning (ML)**, **deep learning (DL)**, and **modern natural language processing (NLP)**—that enable systems to learn from data, interpret complex inputs, generate content, and support automated decision-making.

Topics covered include (but are not limited to):

* Generative AI and foundation models
* Small language models and multimodal systems
* Agentic systems and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)
* Computer vision and reinforcement learning
* Explainable and responsible AI
* Federated and embedded AI
* Neurosymbolic methods and genetic algorithms
* AI for science
* Emerging paradigms such as quantum AI

## How sessions work

Typical formats (we mix them):
- **Talk + discussion** (internal or external speaker)
- **Hands-on session** (small exercises, live coding)
- **Paper or video club** (short prep, deeper discussion)
- **Show and tell** (demos of tools)
- **Clinic session** (bring your problem, we dissect it)
- **Share your solutions** (built something cool? Bring it!)
